Yanqiu Yang

I am an incoming Assistant Professor of Precision Beef Systems & Sustainability at the University of Tennessee. My work focuses on developing AI-powered tools for sustainable agriculture, combining computer vision, sensor integration, and digital platforms.

I recently earned my Ph.D. in Agricultural and Biological Engineering from Penn State University, where I was advised by Prof. Paul Heinemann. My doctoral research centered on precision pest management and plant disease detection, leveraging hyperspectral imaging, machine learning, and smart sensing technologies.

Looking ahead, my research aims to apply these technologies to animal systems, with the goal of building an integrated platform that connects animal behavior, forage quality, and environmental conditions through AI-driven decision support.

I am the founder and AI lead engineer of bioWatch, a mobile platform for invasive species monitoring, and the lead developer of MothNet, a web-based tool for identifying and tracking invasive box tree moths.
